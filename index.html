About

Building the future of autonomous coordination

I am a robotics researcher and engineer with a PhD from Aarhus University's AI in Robotics Lab (AIR Lab), where I spent three years solving one of the core challenges in autonomous systems: how do you get large groups of robots to work together — intelligently, reliably, and without communicating?

My PhD produced four research contributions spanning graph neural networks, Gaussian belief propagation, and multi-agent reinforcement learning — all published or submitted to IROS and ICRA, the top conferences in robotics.

Beyond academia, I engineer complete autonomous systems end-to-end: from custom embedded hardware and firmware, through sensor integration and real-time fusion pipelines, to the software stack that coordinates fleets of over 1,000 robots in live production environments.

Research

My PhD produced four interconnected research pillars — each addressing a fundamental gap in multi-agent coordination for real-world deployment.

IROS 2025 Published · 1st Author
GIANT

Global Path Integration and Attentive Graph Networks for Multi-Agent Trajectory Planning

Robots that navigate complex, crowded environments without ever communicating with each other. GIANT uses an attentive graph neural network to process LiDAR detections of nearby agents, fused with global path guidance — trained end-to-end with noise injection for real-world robustness. Outperforms NH-ORCA, DRL-NAV, and GA3C-CADRL across all tested scenarios.

ICRA 2025 Published · Co-author (supervised)
MAGIC

Multi-Agent Path Planning in Complex Environments using Gaussian Belief Propagation with Global Path Finding

Gaussian Belief Propagation planners are smooth and distributed — but they drift from their intended routes. MAGIC introduces a tracking factor into the GBP factor graph, creating an explicit probabilistic link to the global path. Result: 28% less path deviation and zero inter-robot collisions in structured environments. Secondly it introduces a GBP simulation framework highly customizable for research and education, with support for complex environments, heterogeneous agents, and real-time visualization.

IROS 2023 Published · 1st Author
CAMETA

Conflict-Aware Multi-Agent Estimated Time of Arrival Prediction for Mobile Robots

When a path planner says a robot will arrive in 30 seconds, it's often wrong — because it ignores how agents interact. CAMETA uses a heterogeneous graph neural network to predict when each agent will actually arrive, accounting for conflicts and execution noise. 44% improvement in prediction accuracy over naive baselines.

Adaptive
GBP

Adaptive Weight Tuning in Gaussian Belief Propagation via Graph Neural Networks

GBP planners are sensitive to manually-set parameters — variances that control how much weight each factor gets. Adaptive GBP replaces this with a GNN that reads the robot's local environment and adjusts parameters online: tighter collision avoidance in crowds, stronger path tracking in open space. No manual tuning required.

Simulation & Environments

Built the underwater world.
Then kept going.

I am one of the creators of UNav-Sim — an open-source, photorealistic underwater robotics simulator built on Unreal Engine 5. Designed for vision-based navigation research, it provides a complete stack for perception, SLAM, depth estimation, and control — all bridged to ROS. I designed and built all simulation environments and robot models from scratch, and the simulator has been adopted by labs worldwide for algorithm development and synthetic data generation.
